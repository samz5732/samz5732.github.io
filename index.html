Sr, Beneficiary Claims Anayst

Job Summary

As a Financial Services Representative within the Annuity Beneficiary Claims team, the primary goal is to collaborate with beneficiaries and brokers to determine the most optimal approach for initiating an annuity claim. Beneficiaries can take various forms, including individuals (such as children or spouses), trusts, organizations, or estates, each with distinct claim options and document requirements. A crucial skill for a representative in the beneficiary claims team is the ability to recognize all factors that can influence the available choices for beneficiaries. These factors encompass considerations like whether the contract is Qualified or Non-Qualified, the initiation of required minimum distributions, the beneficiary's relationship to the deceased individual, and whether the deceased person served as the contract owner, annuitant, or both. Additionally, the contract's value and the beneficiary's residency status in the United States play essential roles.

This role demands that representatives possess expertise that extends beyond their specific field, as they work with beneficiaries who have recently experienced a loss. Given the sensitivity of this process, each representative must adeptly handle potential issues that may fall outside their immediate responsibilities. These issues may pertain to matters such as riders, commissions, New Business Products, Withdrawals, Transfers, and a comprehensive understanding of the distinct rules and regulations governing each state and their impact on the claims process. Providing exceptional customer service as a death benefits representative necessitates engagement with various teams within the annuity department to ensure a seamless and supportive experience for beneficiaries.

Tax Specialist

Job Summary

In my role as a tax specialist, my primary duty involved the maintenance of the TaxApp schema within the PMTS02g database. This schema serves as the repository for all tax form information intended for distribution to our annuity customers. The data stored within this schema undergoes validation and is generated in Pallm on a monthly basis. Subsequently, it is transmitted to the database as a batch file. This batch file represents a consolidated summary of transactional data sourced from Pallm, using criteria such as Policy Number, Individual Identification Number, resident State, withdrawal reason code, and group code. This consolidation process condenses several million transactional records into approximately 200,000 form entries. However, it's important to note that this process is not flawless and is susceptible to errors.

My responsibilities encompassed taking this condensed cumulative data and reconciling it with both the PMTS database (Transactional/Non-cumulative) and the Explain Database (Transactional/Non-cumulative) using various technological tools. Furthermore, I was responsible for incorporating known system defects into the monthly reports I generated. Additionally, I provided information and guidance to multiple teams within the operations, service center, and resolution departments.

One of my significant achievements in this role was successfully identifying and resolving a discrepancy in Federal and State withholding amounts, a matter that had garnered the attention of senior management and directors. This discrepancy amounted to an annual imbalance of approximately $500,000.
